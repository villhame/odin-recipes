Channa Masala

Description:

Chickpeas, or "channa" as they are known in Hindi, are the major component in Channa Masala, sometimes referred to as Chickpea Curry, a well-known dish from the Indian subcontinent. Cooked in a tomato-based sauce with a blend of spices, including cumin, coriander, turmeric, ginger, and garlic, chickpeas are a healthy source of protein. It is a well-liked meal in north India and Pakistan and has certain versions that also include onion.

It's a vegetarian dish that's frequently accompanied with rice, naan, or chapati. In order to create varied flavours, Channa Masala can also be made in a variety of ways by including ingredients like coconut milk, tamarind paste, or yoghurt.

It's a tasty and flavorful dish that some areas regard as comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon rice flour
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 cans of chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro
