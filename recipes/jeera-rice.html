Jeera Rice

Description:

Rice with jeera, or cumin, is a straightforward and well-liked meal from the Indian subcontinent. It is prepared by combining cumin seeds, or jeera as they are known in Hindi, with several other common spices including bay leaves, cinnamon, and black pepper. Before being added to the rice, the cumin seeds are dry roasted to release their scent, which gives the meal its distinctive flavour.

Jeera rice is a flexible food that goes well with many different curries, lentil meals, and veggies. It is typically served as a side dish to larger meals and is regarded as a staple item.

Please follow and enjoy the recipe :)

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 cup dry jasmine rice
  • 1 ¾ cups water
  • salt to taste

Steps:

  1. Heat the oil in a medium size saucepan over a medium-high heat. Drop in the cumin seeds, and cook until they splutter. Do not allow the cumin seeds to burn or become really dark brown in color.
  2. Add the rice and fry it in the oil for about 1 minute.
  3. Add the water and salt and bring to a boil. Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the saucepan. Cook the rice for approximately 15 minutes.
  4. If you feel the rice is getting burnt near the base of the pan as it cooks, one trick is to place the saucepan on another flat pan or griddle which is directly on the flame. Toss with a fork.
